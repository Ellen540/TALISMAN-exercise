## Control Design Proposal

To keep the laser spot on the target position, I would use a closed-loop feedback controller based on the estimated spot position from the camera image. As the systems responds smoothly to the actuator commands and shows slow dift over time, the feedback signal would be the position error, defined as the difference between the measured spot coordinates and the target coordinates. An open-llop approach could be considered based on the actuator response measurements from Task 3, but I decieded a closed loop feedback method would be more reliable due to the small flucuations exhibited and the changing operating conditions. Without feedback from the camera, an open-loop controller would not be able to maintain accurate alignment over long periods.

The primary feedback variable would be the laser spot position estimated from a 2D Gaussian fit to the camera image, as this provides both sub-pixel position estimates and an uncertainty measure.The telemetry channels would be treated as supplementary information rather than primary feedback signals. In particular, the photodiode current appears to be correlated with spot motion and could be monitored as a diagnostic indicator of beam quality or alignment. However, because it does not directly measure position, it should not replace image-based feedback. If needed to reduce computaional power, telemetry channels showing little correlation with spot motion could be ignored in the controller as they would not improve performance however it could be nice to keep them as a comparison. For example, vibrations showed little correlation with the spot motion however could be used to identify periods when position estimates are less reliable. 

To handle sudden spot displacements, the controller should continuously monitor the position error and respond with larger corrective commands when the error becomes large. If the spot moves far enough that the normal tracking algorithm loses it, a recovery mode could be used. THis could inlcude something like a spiral search which searchs for the spot until it is reaquired. Actuator limits should be continued to be used to reduce the lieklyhood of excessive movements. 

There will be many practical issues with hardware that could reduce perfomrance so careful consideration and testing is needed to reduce this uncerstainty. Sensor noise and image noise will introduce uncertainty into the position estimate. Thermal drift may change the actuator response over time and mechanical vibrations may also introduce disturbances that cannot be completely removed by feedback.
